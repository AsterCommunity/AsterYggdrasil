# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

We want AsterYggdrasil to be a professional, technically focused, and welcoming project for contributors, users, and maintainers.

Examples of behavior that help build that environment include:

- being respectful in technical discussion, even when disagreeing
- giving feedback that is specific, actionable, and tied to the work
- assuming good intent before escalating conflict
- being considerate of different experience levels, backgrounds, and communication styles
- respecting privacy, boundaries, and project security practices
- keeping discussions on-topic in issues, pull requests, discussions, review comments, and other project spaces

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in this project or its community spaces:

- harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct of any kind
- personal attacks, insults, intimidation, threats, or sustained hostility
- sexualized language, unwanted sexual attention, or inappropriate imagery
- trolling, baiting, deliberate provocation, or repeatedly derailing conversations
- doxxing or sharing someone else's private information without explicit permission
- impersonation, fraud, or intentionally misleading other contributors or users
- spam, abusive advertising, or unrelated self-promotion
- encouraging or coordinating harmful behavior against individuals or the project
- any conduct that would reasonably make participation unsafe, hostile, or exhausting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, review comments, and wiki or documentation contributions
- project chat, email, and other communication channels used for AsterYggdrasil collaboration
- public spaces where someone is representing the project, its maintainers, or its community

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it to:

- `report@esaps.net`

Please include relevant context when possible, such as links, screenshots, timestamps, and a short description of what happened.

For safety and privacy reasons, do not use public GitHub issues or discussions to report conduct incidents involving harassment, abuse, or private information.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for reviewing reports and deciding an appropriate response.

Possible actions include:

- asking for a change in behavior
- editing or removing comments, posts, or other content
- locking threads or ending discussions
- rejecting contributions associated with the behavior
- temporary suspension from project spaces
- permanent ban from participation in project spaces

Maintainers will review reports in good faith, aim to protect affected people, and handle personal information as confidentially as reasonably possible.

## Enforcement Principles

When evaluating a report, maintainers will consider:

- the severity of the behavior
- whether the conduct was repeated or targeted
- the impact on community safety and collaboration
- whether an immediate protective action is necessary
